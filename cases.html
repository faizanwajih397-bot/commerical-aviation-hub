About Commercial Aircraft

Commercial aircraft are engineered to carry passengers and cargo safely, efficiently, and reliably across every distance — from short regional hops to nonstop flights spanning entire hemispheres. Two manufacturers, Boeing and Airbus, dominate this market, each offering a family of narrow-body and wide-body jets tailored to different route networks. Advances in aerodynamics, composite materials, and engine technology have made modern aircraft dramatically quieter and more fuel-efficient than the generation before them, while digital fly-by-wire flight controls and advanced avionics continue to raise the bar for safety and precision.

The iconic humpbacked jumbo jet that pioneered wide-body air travel and long remained the world's largest passenger aircraft.

Airbus Aircraft

AIRBUS

Airbus A220

Originally the Bombardier CSeries, the A220 is a highly efficient small narrow-body designed for regional and thin long-haul routes.

  • Capacity108–160 passengers
  • Range3,350 nmi
  • Cruise SpeedMach 0.78
  • First Flight2013
AIRBUS

Airbus A320 Family

Airbus's best-selling narrow-body line — including the A319, A320, and A321neo — competes directly with the Boeing 737 on short and medium-haul routes.

  • Capacity140–244 passengers
  • Range3,300–4,000 nmi
  • Cruise SpeedMach 0.78
  • First Flight1987
AIRBUS

Airbus A330

A dependable wide-body twinjet offering strong economics on medium to long-haul routes, popular for both passenger and freighter use.

  • Capacity247–300 passengers
  • Range7,250 nmi
  • Cruise SpeedMach 0.82
  • First Flight1992
AIRBUS

Airbus A340

A four-engine long-range widebody built for routes far from diversion airports, later succeeded by more efficient twinjets.

  • Capacity261–380 passengers
  • Range7,900 nmi
  • Cruise SpeedMach 0.82
  • First Flight1991
AIRBUS

Airbus A350 XWB

An extra-wide-body composite jet built to rival the 787 and 777, delivering excellent range with class-leading passenger comfort.

  • Cruise SpeedMach 0.85

AIRBUS

Airbus A380

The world's largest passenger airliner, a full double-deck superjumbo built for the busiest high-capacity long-haul routes.
