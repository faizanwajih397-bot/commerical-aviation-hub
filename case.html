Industry Overview

About Commercial Aviation

Commercial aviation is the sector of air transport concerned with operating aircraft for hire to carry passengers and cargo. Since the first scheduled passenger flight took off in 1914, the industry has grown into a vast global network of airlines, airports, and manufacturers that moves billions of people every year. Regulated by international bodies such as ICAO and IATA, commercial aviation blends cutting-edge engineering with complex logistics — coordinating air traffic control, ground operations, and maintenance across thousands of routes to keep the world connected safely and efficiently.

Rich History

From early biplanes to the jet age, commercial aviation has continuously reinvented how the world travels.

Global Regulation

ICAO and IATA set the safety, security, and operational standards that airlines follow worldwide.

Route Networks

Hub-and-spoke systems built around major airports allow efficient connections between thousands of cities.
